Effective waste management practices are vital for several reasons. First, they minimize the pollution of air, water, and soil that can result from improper waste disposal. For example, open landfills can contaminate drinking water sources and spread diseases. Second, waste management practices can help recover valuable resources through recycling and composting, reducing the need to extract new raw materials. Third, reducing waste and promoting recycling creates jobs in the waste management sector and related industries. Finally, effective waste management contributes to a circular economy, where resources are reused and recycled, minimizing environmental impact and maximizing resource efficiency
